Planetary Rovers

Across distant worlds, they move — slowly, silently, searching.

Planetary rovers are robotic explorers designed to travel across the surfaces of other worlds. They analyze terrain, collect samples, and search for signs of life — acting as humanity's hands and eyes beyond Earth.

Each wheel turn is a command sent across millions of kilometers. Each photo, a postcard from another world. Each rock examined, a question asked of a planet that has kept its secrets for four billion years.

Mars rovers have collectively driven over 100 kilometers on another planet. Every centimeter was earned — planned days in advance, executed with caution, and confirmed hours after it happened.

ROVER — 001 / SOJOURNER

The First Steps

Sojourner was the first rover to operate on another planet. It proved that mobile exploration on Mars was possible, marking a new era in robotic space missions — and changing what we believed we could do.

Roughly the size of a microwave oven, it carried cameras and an alpha proton X-ray spectrometer, analyzing the chemical composition of rocks no human hand had ever touched.

ROVER — 002 / SPIRIT & OPPORTUNITY

The Twin Explorers

These twin rovers explored Mars far beyond their expected 90-sol lifetimes. Spirit operated for 6 years; Opportunity for nearly 15 — a machine that was never supposed to survive a single Martian winter.

They discovered strong evidence that liquid water once flowed across the Martian surface — a finding that fundamentally changed our understanding of Mars' past and the possibility of life.

ROVER — 003 / CURIOSITY

A Mobile Laboratory

Curiosity is a car-sized rover equipped with 17 cameras and 10 advanced scientific instruments. It studies Mars' climate and geology, searching for conditions that could have supported microbial life.

Nuclear-powered, it does not depend on sunlight to operate — allowing it to survive Martian winters and dust storms that silenced its solar-powered predecessors forever.

ROVER — 004 / PERSEVERANCE

Seeking Ancient Life

Perseverance is the most capable rover ever sent to another world. It is designed specifically to search for signs of ancient microbial life, collecting rock and soil samples that may one day be returned to Earth.

It also carried Ingenuity — a small helicopter that achieved the first powered flight on another planet. What the Wright Brothers did for Earth, Ingenuity did for Mars.

ROVER — 005 / LUNAR ROVERS

Driving on the Moon

Used during Apollo missions 15, 16, and 17, the Lunar Roving Vehicle allowed astronauts to explore the Moon's surface far beyond walking distance — covering terrain that would have been unreachable on foot.

Electric-powered, it carried the astronauts, their tools, and rock samples across the lunar regolith at up to 18 km/h — the only crewed vehicle ever driven on another world.

The last human to drive on another world was Eugene Cernan, during Apollo 17 in December 1972. No human has driven on the Moon — or any other world — since.
